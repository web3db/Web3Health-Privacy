Web3Health

UGA SensorWeb Lab

Research-backed • Privacy-first • User-controlled sharing

A research-backed app that lets users collect and share their wearable health metrics with participating studies using privacy-first, blockchain-enabled research infrastructure.

Web3Health is part of an ongoing academic research project conducted by the UGA SensorWeb Lab. Features and data-handling workflows are actively being refined to support ethical, privacy-aware research participation.

What the app does

  • Securely reads health metrics you consent to share (e.g., steps, distance, heart rate, sleep, calories).
  • Lets you opt into specific sharing opportunities with universities, research studies, or trusted buyers.
  • Uploads only the windows of data you approve; you can pause or cancel at any time.
  • Stores records on a secure backend; sensitive data benefits from HTTPS in transit and managed encryption at rest.

Who is it for?

  • Participants in SensorWeb Lab studies.
  • Individuals who want granular control over their wearable data.
  • Partners who value consent, transparency, and auditability.

Permissions used

  • Health data access — to read metrics (steps, heart rate, sleep, active calories) you approve.
  • Background tasks — to support timely syncing of approved data windows, subject to platform limitations.
  • Notifications — to remind you when a window is due or if attention is needed.
  • Network access — to securely upload data to our servers.

You can revoke any permission later in your device settings.

Data safety at a glance

  • ✔ Data in transit is protected with HTTPS (TLS). Stored data uses Supabase’s managed database encryption and access controls.
  • ✔ We only collect data categories needed to run the app and fulfill your choices.
  • ✔ You can request account deletion and data export at any time via support; deletion requests are handled manually.
  • ✔ We do not sell your personal data.
